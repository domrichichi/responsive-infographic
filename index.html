The Future of Fitness 2015

Current State of the Fitness-Tech Industry

Consumers increasingly turn to their smart phones and activity trackers when looking to improve their health and fitness

While consumer fitness tracking products are on the rise, the fitness industry has been slow to adopt a solution for the in-gym experience

Three Areas of Opportunities for Connected Gyms

1. Motivated members lead to higher retention

2. Virtual Personal Training creates a new revenue stream for gyms

Personal training is inaccessible to many members at an average costs of $56 per session

3. Gym analytics provide new insights into what equipment members need the most

What’s Holding Back Connected Gyms

The variety of exercises that can be performed in the gym mean that there is no one-size-fits-all sensor for tracking everything. Rather, there needs to be a suite of sensors for different equipment and exercises, and a way to connect all the systems

The fitness industry is ready for this technological leap. With products like Gymtrack spearheading this change, IoT enabled solutions are an inevitable element of the gym of the future

Why the Internet of Things (IoT) Will Change the Fitness Industry

As of 2008, there are now more “things or objects” connected to the Internet than people. Already, companies across industries are seeing positive returns on their IoT investments, however, the fitness industry has yet to fully capitalize on the increased opportunity
